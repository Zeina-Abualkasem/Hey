a letter

for Zeina

— in the quiet light of apology —

I made you feel less than you are,
and that is not something I can undo with words alone.

But I want you to know: I see you.
Not as an idea, not as a reflection of me —
but as the person who makes pink feel like a serious color.

I am sorry for the distance I created.
I am sorry for not being present.

“Poetry was invented to describe you —
but even the best verses stumble
at the weight of your stillness.”
